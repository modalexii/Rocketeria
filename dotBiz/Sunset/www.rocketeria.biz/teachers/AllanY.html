Allan Young

 

Guitar, Bass, Mandolin, Tenor Banjo, Ukulele, Piano & Drums (Beginner)

 

When did you start?

 

When I was about nine—I had to learn on an old Rockoman nylon string guitar that was bigger than me for three years before I got my first electric.
 

Who are your influences?

 

My first influence was my dad. He taught me the rudiments of classical music and then later on, he turned me on to jazz guitar greats such as Django Reinhardt, John McLaughlin, Wes Montgomery, and Joe Pass.

 

As I got a little older, I got turned on to punk and metal—despite my parents best efforts to steer me away from it. 

 

I still think that when it comes to songwriting, the Beatles set the bar and are the go-to for any young student who hasn't been exposed to much popular music.
 

What's your musical background—where and who have you played with?

 

I grew up playing a lot of big band jazz in school and as I got a little older, I played classic rock with people almost twice my age (at the time)—they had to drive me to and from rehearsals.

 

For a long time I lead a double life. On one hand, I was in Starfire—a high–society band playing old school R&B and jazz standards throughout the Washington D.C area. On the other, I was in Gorman Prophecy, which was once declared

Baltimore's best metal band by (then) 98 rock radio personality Kirk McEwen.

We toured up and down the east coast and were briefly signed to Screaming Ferret Wreckords.


When did you first consider yourself a professional musician?

 

When I was able to make enough money (through both teaching and playing)

to support myself.
 

Who do you admire? 

 

Belinda, my wife.

 

Also Dave and Ariana for taking a mom & pop music store and making it

as successful as it has been for almost 10 years with a lot of creative

outside-the-box thinking.

 

What's your favorite part of teaching? 

 

Seeing students reach that 'break-out moment' when things click, and from then on, they start to progress in leaps and bounds.

 

I have also enjoyed seeing my student jam bands come together and progress the way they have during the past year.
 

Who are your favorite artists?

 

These days I find myself listening to a lot of punk based bands like Rancid, Bad Religion, Rise Against, Offspring, Zeke, NOFX, Bad Brains, and some crossover

bands like Avenged Sevenfold and Falling In Reverse.

 

Additionally, I like a ton of 70's and 90's rock, classic metal, and even some jazz and country.
