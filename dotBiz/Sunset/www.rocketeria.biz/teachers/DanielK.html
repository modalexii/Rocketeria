Daniel Kehoe

 

Bass, Double Bass, Guitar + Beginner Piano, Drums, Saxophone, Clarinet

 

When did you start?

 

I started with teaching myself some piano in the 3rd grade and by the 4th grade I was playing clarinet in band class. In 6th grade I started playing guitar and bass but took more of a liking to the bass and that's when the adventure really began.

 

In high school I began playing the drums and saxophone. Once i got to college I began studying Jazz and the upright bass as well as exploring the realms of electronic music, music production and recording.

 

Who are your influences?

 

My biggest influences in music come mainly from composers and bass players such as Geddy Lee, Paul McCartney, Charles Mingus, Arnold Schoenberg, and David Bowie. Outside of music I'm influenced by my family, friends and nature. 

 

What's your musical background—where and who have you played with?

 

I have been studying bass for 10 years now and gig around mostly as a bass player. I have played shows around Baltimore at places like the Ottobar, the Depot and An Die Musik as well as the Rock and Roll Hotel and the Fillmore in Silver Spring. I also have had the pleasure of playing at Rocketeria's Rock the Lot with my high school band TCPP. I was also a member of the local metal band The Enigma Code and have played with other groups like Michelle McTierney and the Whiskys, The Don Arnold Big Band and the World Famous Towson Marching Band. I also occasionally freelance on Upright Bass, playing jazz anywhere from farmers markets to retirement homes.


When did you first consider yourself a professional musician?

 

Once I chose to study music in college I knew that if I want to take music on seriously, I would have to have a professional attitude when it came to any and all music so I opened myself up to all the opportunities and decreed myself a professional musician!

 

Who do you admire? 

 

All of the teachers that I've had in music starting with Allan Young who got me started as a bass player. And then Dave Demarco who took my playing and appreciation for bass and music to the next level. Alex Silverbook, who introduced me to jazz and inspired me to study music in college.

 

In college Jeff Reed, who was my bass professor helped me obtain a better, deeper understanding of jazz and the bass. And Tim Murphy and Thomas Ciufo who helped me explore the realms of composition and making music.

 

What's your favorite part of teaching? 

 

My favorite part about teaching is keeping the tradition of personal care and touch alive. In today's day and age where you can practically teach yourself with the help of the internet and technology, I think the personal touch of having one-on-one time with an actual person can be much more effective and influential.

 

Who are your favorite artists?

 

Rush, Green Day, The Beatles (and anything Paul McCartney), John Coltrane, Charles Mingus, The Grateful Dead, The Doors, Thelonious Monk, David Bowie.
