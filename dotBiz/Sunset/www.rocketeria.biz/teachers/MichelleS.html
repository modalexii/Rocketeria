Michelle Salah

 

Voice, Guitar (Beginner), poproxx

 

When did you start?

 

I started singing from a very young age at home. At about age seven I started to belt classic Disney and Barbie movie songs as my dad recorded me for our home videos. I always knew I loved to sing but at around the age 12 or 13 people started noticing I had a good voice and told me to join choir. I joined my freshman year of high school and continued to do it for the rest of my four years. Ever since then I involved myself in all things singing!

 

Who are your influences?

 

My biggest influence is my mom, who has an amazing voice. She sang mostly church hymns and lullabies to my sisters and I. Some artists I consider to be my idols include Kelly Clarkson, Carrie Underwood, Miranda Lambert, and Rascal Flatts. I mostly listen to country and pop music, as well as musical theater songs.

 

What's your musical background—where and who have you played with?

 

My first solo performance ever was singing the national anthem for the Pom competition my team was hosting during my freshman year. After that I gained the confidence I needed to audition for my school musical and chamber choir, both which I made.

 

In choir, I was continuously given solos and I was asked to be the singer from then on, each year for my grade's homecoming float. Since then I have collaborated with many of my musical friends to create original works.

 

My senior year I was given my first lead as Addeperale in the musical “The Wiz”. Once I graduated I joined theater at Montgomery College, getting leads or featured rolls in multiple shows.


When did you first consider yourself a professional musician?

 

I first considered myself a professional musician when I choose to be a theater major and decided to dedicate my summers to performing with the Montgomery College Summer dinner theater!

 

Who do you admire? 

 

I admire my Mom because she has a beautiful voice and because she introduced music and singing to me at a very young age.

 

What's your favorite part of teaching? 

 

I love to help students learn how to do something that I am so passionate about. I also enjoy seeing students show pride in their accomplishments!

 

Who are your favorite artists?

 

Some of my favorite artists, aside from those I previously mentioned include: Tim McGraw, Taylor Swift, Halsey, Miley Cyrus, Keith Urban, Demi Lovato, and Chance the Rapper!
