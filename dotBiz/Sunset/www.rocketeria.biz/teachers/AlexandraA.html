Alexandra August

 

Voice & Piano (Beginner), poproxx

 

When did you start?

 

I started training my voice in middle school when I became friends with a girl who was part of a musical theatre group. Watching her perform onstage inspired me to be onstage as well. I was always quiet when I was younger so developing my singing voice was a great outlet for expressing myself.

 

Who are your influences?

 

I would definitely say the pop singer, Celine Dion. I love how she communicates so much emotion through her singing and has so much range and power. She also has a very silly personality and is very kind.

 

What's your musical background—where and who have you played with?

 

I’ve sung in the UMD Concert Choir where I performed with the National Symphony Orchestra and the Baltimore Symphony Orchestra. I’ve also performed solo roles with community opera companies and programs in the area. 

 

When did you first consider yourself a professional musician?

 

The University of Maryland, College Park's rigorous music program really helped my growth as a musician. It was hard, but definitely worth it. I think after I graduated from their program and started  performing more in the area and teaching, I started considering myself as a professional musician.

 

Who do you admire? 

 

I admire the voice teacher I studied with in high school. He has had a lot of challenges that he overcame during his singing career and hasn’t let anything get him down.

 

What's your favorite part of teaching? 

 

I love seeing my students improve and grow in confidence. 

 

Who are your favorite artists?

 

Anna Netrebko, Natalie Dessay, Celine Dion, Bonnie Tyler, Pat Benatar, Frankie Valli, John Denver, Enya, Jim Croce, and Michael Ball.
