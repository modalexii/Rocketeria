Brian Waitzman

 

Guitar, Bass, Piano, Ukulele

 

When did you start?

 

I started playing guitar in 6th grade, and took up the piano in high school. My friend played bass, so we taught each other how to play, and then we started a band together. I've been obsessed with music ever since!

 

Who are your influences?

 

My earliest influence was The Talking Heads. My family had copies of (the movies) Stop Making Sense, and True Stories. I watched them over and over...As I started playing guitar I got into 90's rock, 70's funk, and classic rock.

 

Some of my influences are James Brown, P-Funk, Pink Floyd, The Sex Pistols, Jimi Hendrix, Stone Temple Pilots, Red Hot Chili Peppers, Phish, Led Zep, Tool, The Beatles, Michael Jackson, Pantera, Curtis Mayfield, David Bowie, The Ohio Players, Metallica, Dr. Dre and Snoop Dogg, The Postal Service, System of a Down, Beck, Coldplay, Fishbone and hundreds more! 

 

What's your musical background - where and who have you played with?

 

My musical journey began in Philadelphia with guitar lessons at school, and with private teachers. My friends and I began jamming together as soon as we could, and I also spent a lot of time playing along with my favorite songs. That's really how I got good; by studying, jamming in the basement with my buddies, and playing along to records.

 

I came to D.C. to study recording engineering at The Omega Studios School of Recording Arts and Sciences, where I earned two certificates, and I spent time interning at the studio as well. Through the studio, playing in bands, and working at a record store I became part of the D.C. music scene. I've happily been here ever since!

 

I currently perform with The Fishermen Band, Highway Star,

and singer songwriter Flo Anito.

 

When did you first consider yourself a professional musician?

 

I've been a pro musician ever since I first stepped off stage and was handed a wad of cash! But seriously, teaching at Rocketeria and performing on stage, and having no "day job" over the last 7 years has taken me to the full time professional level. 

 

Who do you admire?

 

I admire really courageous artists who blaze a path for themselves and create honest artwork. I'm currently obsessed with HAIM, Bat For Lashes, Animals As Leaders, Grimes, Bo Burnam, and Robyn. All these artists inspire me to be the truest, and most musical version of myself. 

 

What's your favorite part of teaching?

 

Giving students the opportunity to learn the way they want to, sharing all my passion with them, and hearing all the music they love. 

 

Who are your favorite artists?

 

Some of my favorite bands are artists my students have introduced to me!

As a teacher I believe the more you give me, the more you get out of me. I personally love that moment when you see someone "get it",

and I consider it an honor to be a part of that process.
